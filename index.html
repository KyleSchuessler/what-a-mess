Transgender Cultural Competency
Addressing the unique needs of the transgender client

"Cultural humility is a lifelong process of self-reflection, self-critique and commitment to understanding and respecting different points of view, and engaging with others humbly, authentically and from a place of learning." (Tervalon & Murray-Garcia, 1998).

What We Offer and Benefits of Our Training:

  • Trans cultural competency training
  • Greater familiarity with language surrounding gender and transition
  • Best practices in regard to treating transgender clients
  • How to facilitate restroom comfort
  • Best practices for housing trans patients in care settings
  • Body image concerns in trans and gender-diverse patients or clients
  • Insight into why trans and non-binary folks are at greater risk for developing eating disorders
  • How to address issues facing folks from marginalized communities
  • The changing nutritional needs for folks undergoing medical transition
  • Integrating support for gender issues into your practice or facility
  • Trans support systems and chosen families
  • How to make your research more gender-inclusive
  • Making intake forms gender-literate
  • Insurance and financing obstacles facing trans and gender non-conforming clients
  • Making your services more accessible to trans folks
  • A guide to continuing and deepening cultural competency
  • How to train new hirees/other staff members in gender literacy
  • Visibility and publicity after completion of our training
  • Building relationships with your local queer/trans communities
  • Links to other trans and non-binary resources for you and your clients
  • Ongoing support regarding practice reform
